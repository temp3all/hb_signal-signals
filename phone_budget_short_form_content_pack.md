## Sample mini-pack: security/privacy tool

Product type: end-to-end encrypted file/secret sharing tool.

### Hooks
1. "Most secret-sharing tools still ask you to trust their backend. This one tries not to have a backend to trust."
2. "If your security product needs a diagram to feel safe, your landing page has a trust problem."
3. "The best privacy pitch is not 'we care about privacy.' It's: here is exactly what we cannot see."

### Short post

Most privacy tools say "secure by design."

A stronger launch message is:

"The secret is encrypted in your browser. The server never receives the plaintext. Here is the threat model, here is what can still go wrong, and here is when you should not use it."

That phrasing does two things:
- tells technical users where trust is placed
- makes the product feel more honest, not less

For security products, clarity is conversion.

### 30-second video script

**0-3s caption:** "Your privacy tool has a trust problem."

Visual: blurred landing page full of generic "secure" claims.

**3-10s:** "If users cannot tell what your server can see, they assume the worst."

Visual: server icon with question marks.

**10-20s:** "Better copy: 'Encrypted in your browser. Server receives ciphertext only. No account. No stored plaintext.'"

Visual: browser lock -> ciphertext box -> server.

**20-30s:** "Then add the honest limit: phishing, compromised device, and bad passphrases are still user risks. That honesty builds trust."

Visual: checklist: what we protect / what we don't.
